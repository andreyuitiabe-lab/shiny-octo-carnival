# Land Wholesaling & Flipping — Operator Playbook

> Your end-to-end guide to running the business. Read this first, then use the
> **Due Diligence Checklist** (`02-due-diligence-checklist.md`) on every parcel,
> and the **Deal Analyzer** app to run the numbers.

---

## 1. The business in one paragraph

You find owners of vacant land who want to sell, get a parcel under contract
(or under option) at well below market value, then make money one of three ways:

| Exit | What you do | Cash needed | Speed | Profit shape |
|------|-------------|-------------|-------|--------------|
| **Wholesale (assign)** | Assign your purchase contract to an end buyer for a fee. Never take title. | Almost none | Fast | Small–medium, fast |
| **Flip (buy & resell)** | Actually buy the land, resell it higher for cash. | Full purchase price | Medium | Medium–large |
| **Seller finance** | Buy low, resell on monthly payments (you're the bank). | Purchase price | Slow payout | Largest over time, recurring |

Most operators start with **wholesaling** (lowest risk), then graduate to
**flipping**, then build a **seller-finance note portfolio** for recurring income.

## 2. The deal pipeline (the whole business is this funnel)

```
1. PICK A MARKET      → choose a county to farm
2. BUILD A LIST       → owners likely to sell (absentee, tax-delinquent)
3. SKIP TRACE         → get their phone / mailing address
4. MARKET             → send offers (mail / call / text) at volume
5. INTAKE LEADS       → seller responds; qualify them
6. DESKTOP DILIGENCE  → free checks; kill bad parcels fast   ← Analyzer + Checklist
7. PRICE & OFFER      → comps → market value → max offer      ← Deal Analyzer
8. CONTRACT           → get it under purchase agreement / option
9. DEEP DILIGENCE     → title, access, flood, zoning (costs $) ← Checklist
10. FIND BUYER        → list it; or assign to investor
11. CLOSE             → title company / attorney handles deed & money
12. REPEAT            → reinvest, scale the list
```

> **Rule of the funnel:** stages 1–7 should be cheap or free. You only spend
> real money at stages 8–11, and only on parcels that already passed. This is
> what keeps you from tying up cash in unusable dirt.

## 3. Stage-by-stage detail

### Stage 1 — Pick a market
You want a county that is:
- **Affordable** (parcels in the low thousands, so margins are forgiving)
- **Has demand** — people actually buy rural/recreational/buildable lots there
- **Has a usable GIS site** (county parcel map — you'll live in it)
- Not your backyard necessarily; many operators farm counties they've never visited.

Signals of demand: active listings on LandWatch / Lands of America, recent sold
comps, population or recreation growth nearby.

### Stage 2 — Build a list
Pull a list of owners statistically more likely to sell cheap:
- **Absentee / out-of-state owners** — bought "someday" land, never used it.
- **Tax-delinquent owners** — behind on property taxes, motivated.
- **Inherited / long-held vacant parcels.**
- Filter by acreage and land-use code to match your strategy.

Sources: PropStream, DataTree, Regrid, county tax assessor delinquent lists.

### Stage 3 — Skip trace
Turn "owner name" into phone numbers and a verified mailing address.
Tools: BatchSkipTracing, TLOxp, IDI/LexisNexis.

### Stage 4 — Market (the volume game)
This is the engine. Most land businesses are **direct-mail-driven**:
- Send **neutral / blind offer letters** — often a specific dollar offer at
  ~25–50% of your estimated market value. Lowball on purpose; the few who say
  yes are your deals.
- Supplement with **cold calling and texting** for speed.
- It's a numbers game: response rates are low (often 1–5%), so you mail at volume
  and let the Deal Analyzer filter the responses.

### Stage 5 — Intake & qualify
When a seller responds, capture: parcel ID (APN), county, why they're selling,
their price expectation, any known issues, whether taxes are current. Log it in
your pipeline. Be friendly and low-pressure — you're a buyer doing them a favor.

### Stage 6 — Desktop diligence (FREE — kills 80% of bad deals)
From your desk, before spending a dime, check the deal-killers:
- **Access** — does it touch a public road, or is it landlocked?
- **Zoning / permitted use** — can the buyer actually use it?
- **Flood zone** (FEMA) and **wetlands** (USFWS).
- **Topography** — buildable, or a cliff/swamp?
- **Utilities** nearby (power/water) or feasible (septic/well)?
- **Back taxes / liens** owed.

See `02-due-diligence-checklist.md`. The Deal Analyzer has these as gates.

### Stage 7 — Price & make the offer
1. Pull **3–6 sold comps** of similar parcels (size, area, use).
2. Compute **price per acre** → estimate this parcel's **market value (MV)**.
3. Decide exit and target resale price.
4. Subtract costs and your desired profit → **Maximum Allowable Offer (MAO)**.
5. Offer at or below MAO.

The **Deal Analyzer app does this math for you.**

### Stage 8 — Get it under contract
Use a **purchase agreement** with an **assignment clause** (for wholesaling) or
an **option agreement**. Include an **inspection/due-diligence period** so you
can back out if deep diligence finds a problem. A real estate attorney or title
company can give you a state-appropriate template — don't freelance contracts.

### Stage 9 — Deep diligence (now you spend a little)
- **Title search** (title company/attorney) — liens, mortgages, judgments.
- **Easements & encroachments.**
- **HOA / deed restrictions (CC&Rs).**
- **Mineral / water rights** (sometimes severed).
- **Survey** — only if boundaries are unclear (it costs money — do it last).

### Stage 10 — Find your buyer
- **Assign:** market the contract to your investor buyer list.
- **Flip:** list on LandWatch, Lands of America, Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist,
  Land.com, your own Carrot site. Use **Land id** to make a shareable parcel map.
- Validate demand with recent sold comps and days-on-market.

### Stage 11 — Close
A **title company or closing attorney** does the title work, prepares the deed,
and handles the money (escrow). For an assignment, your buyer closes and you get
your assignment fee at closing. For seller-finance resale, the closing sets up
the note and you collect monthly.

### Stage 12 — Repeat & scale
Reinvest profit into bigger mail volume and better lists. Track your numbers:
mail sent → leads → contracts → closings → profit. Optimize the weakest stage.

## 4. Key numbers / rules of thumb

- **Offer price:** land wholesalers often offer **25–50% of market value** in the
  blind offer; lock deals where there's a real spread.
- **Flip margin:** aim to buy at a price that lets you resell at a profit even
  after a price cut to sell fast — many target buying at **40–60% of MV**.
- **Response rate** on cold mail: often **1–5%**. Plan volume accordingly.
- **Costs to budget:** back taxes owed, closing/title (~$300–$1,500 on cheap
  land), recording fees, holding (taxes while you own it), marketing/listing.

> These are starting heuristics, not gospel. Your county's data refines them.

---

## 5. The #1 way beginners lose money

**Tying up capital in unusable land.** Landlocked parcels, wetlands, flood
zones, steep unbuildable slopes, parcels with back taxes bigger than the land's
worth, or title clouds. **Every one of these is catchable from your desk for
free in Stage 6.** Discipline at the free stage is the whole game.

---

## 6. Legal / licensing note (read this)

- **Wholesaling (assigning contracts) is regulated differently by state.** A few
  states have tightened rules; some situations look like acting as an unlicensed
  broker. Know your state's rules.
- Use **attorney- or title-company-provided contract templates**.
- This playbook is education, **not legal advice.** Before you operate, talk to a
  local real estate attorney and/or title company in your target state.
