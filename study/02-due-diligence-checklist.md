# Due Diligence Checklist — Vacant Land

> Run this on **every** parcel. Cheapest checks first so you kill bad deals for
> free. Each item notes the free tool to use. The Deal Analyzer app turns these
> into pass/fail gates that drive a GO / CAUTION / KILL verdict.

Legend: 🟢 = free desktop check · 🟡 = small cost · 🔴 = costs real money (do last)

---

## Stage A — Identity & money owed (🟢 free, do first)

- [ ] **Confirm legal ownership** — seller's name matches county records (tax assessor / recorder).
- [ ] **Legal description & APN** — Assessor's Parcel Number matches what you're buying.
- [ ] **Acreage & dimensions** — county record matches the listing/seller's claim.
- [ ] **Back property taxes** — pull the tax bill. How much is owed? (Could exceed land value.)
- [ ] **Obvious liens / tax certificates** — note anything flagged in assessor/recorder data.

> Tools: county **Tax Assessor** site, county **Recorder/Clerk** site, PropStream/Regrid.

---

## Stage B — Can the land actually be used? (🟢 free — the deal-killers)

This stage catches the #1 newbie mistake: buying unusable dirt.

- [ ] **Legal access** — does the parcel touch a **public road**? Use county GIS + Google Earth.
  - ⚠️ **Landlocked** = no public road frontage = needs an easement. Usually a KILL or heavy discount.
- [ ] **Physical access** — is there an actual way in (existing road/trail), not just legal access?
- [ ] **Zoning / land-use code** — what does the county allow here? Residential? Ag? Recreational?
- [ ] **Minimum lot size & setbacks** — can a buyer build given the parcel size?
- [ ] **Flood zone** — check **FEMA Flood Map Service Center**. Zone A / AE = expensive, harder to sell.
- [ ] **Wetlands** — check **USFWS National Wetlands Inventory**. Wetlands ≈ can't develop.
- [ ] **Topography / slope** — Google Earth / USGS topo. Steep, cliff, ravine = unbuildable.
- [ ] **Utilities — power** — how far is the nearest line? "2 miles to power" tanks value.
- [ ] **Utilities — water** — city water available, or well needed?
- [ ] **Utilities — sewer/septic** — sewer hookup, or is the soil suitable for septic (perc test)?
- [ ] **Environmental red flags** — old dump, contamination, protected habitat nearby.

> Tools: county **GIS parcel viewer**, **Google Earth Pro**, **FEMA MSC**, **USFWS NWI**, **Regrid / Land id**.

---

## Stage C — Market & exit validation (🟢 free, run in parallel)

- [ ] **Sold comps** — find 3–6 similar parcels SOLD recently (size, area, use).
- [ ] **Price per acre** — compute average; estimate this parcel's market value.
- [ ] **Active demand** — how many similar lots are listed? How long do they sit (days on market)?
- [ ] **Decide exit** — wholesale (assign) / flip / seller-finance.
- [ ] **There IS a margin** — your max offer leaves room for profit after costs. (Deal Analyzer.)

> Tools: **LandWatch / Lands of America**, **Zillow (sold filter)**, **Redfin**, county sold records, **AcreValue**.

---

## Stage D — Title & encumbrances (🔴 spend money — only on parcels that passed A–C)

- [ ] **Title search** — order from a **title company** or **closing attorney**. Find mortgages, liens, judgments.
- [ ] **Easements** — utility, pipeline, access easements crossing the parcel.
- [ ] **Encroachments** — neighbor's structure/fence over the line.
- [ ] **HOA / POA** — dues owed, restrictions, approval requirements.
- [ ] **Deed restrictions / CC&Rs** — limits on use, building type, mobile homes, etc.
- [ ] **Mineral rights** — severed? (Common in some states; matters to some buyers.)
- [ ] **Water rights** — relevant in Western/arid states.

> Tools: **title company / real estate attorney** (do not skip — they protect you at closing).

---

## Stage E — Survey & physical (🔴 only if needed, do last)

- [ ] **Survey** — order ONLY if boundaries/legal description are unclear or a lender requires it.
- [ ] **Boundary markers** — corners locatable on the ground.
- [ ] **Perc test** — if rural residential and septic is the plan and it's a higher-value deal.

---

## Verdict logic (how the app decides)

| Condition | Verdict |
|-----------|---------|
| Any **Stage B deal-killer** fails (landlocked, wetland, flood-locked, no buildability) | 🔴 **KILL** |
| Diligence mostly clear but **margin too thin** or **unknowns remain** | 🟡 **CAUTION** — investigate before contract |
| All gates pass + margin above your target | 🟢 **GO** — make the offer |

> Honest data in = honest verdict out. Mark unknowns as **unknown**, not as pass.
> An unknown deal-killer should hold you at CAUTION until you've checked it.

---

## Quick desktop-screen mini-checklist (the 5-minute version)

Before you spend any real time, answer these five. A "no/bad" on any one is
usually a fast pass:

1. **Access?** Touches a public road? → if no, probably KILL.
2. **Buildable?** Reasonable slope, not all wetland/flood? → if no, KILL.
3. **Zoning OK** for a real use? → if no, KILL.
4. **Back taxes** less than the land is worth? → if no, KILL.
5. **Is there a margin** at 25–50% of market value? → if no, pass.
