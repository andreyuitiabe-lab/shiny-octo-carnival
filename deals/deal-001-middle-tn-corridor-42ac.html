Snapshot verdict

This is not a cheap-dirt wholesale lead — it's a potential developer-grade deal. An owner responded to outreach offering to sell ~42 acres total: an ~9-acre home parcel (house built early 1970s) plus an adjacent ~33-acre vacant lot, fronting a US highway in a fast-growing corridor between two booming Middle Tennessee towns (major master-planned communities breaking ground nearby). Even at conservative rural land values the assemblage is plausibly worth $800K–$1.5M+ before counting the house. Handle with the big-deal playbook: no blind lowball, longer diligence, developer buyer pool, attorney before contract.

3 · Why this deal is different (big-deal playbook)

  • No blind lowball. A 30%-of-value opener on a $1M+ property ends the relationship. Anchor with facts and comps instead.
  • The buyer is a developer/builder or land fund, not a $20K recreational-lot investor. Thinner buyer pool, much bigger fee potential.
  • Structure matters: consider a longer inspection period or an option agreement to shop it to developers. Spend the $300 on a TN real-estate attorney before signing anything.
  • Tennessee wholesaling rules: market your contractual interest only, never the property as if you own it (playbook §10).
  • House complicates it: land-only vs house-included changes value, buyer type, and possibly your exit. First question of the callback.

4 · Callback plan — questions in order

  1. House included, or land only?
  2. Parcel numbers — “The back lot has no address; can you grab the parcel number off the tax bill? Or I can find it on the county map if you tell me where it sits.”
  3. Same name on both deeds? (Spouse / estate / trust — everyone signs.)
  4. Why selling, what timeline? Motivation = discount room.
  5. Access & utilities — does the vacant lot touch the highway or any road itself, or only connect through the house parcel? Well/septic or city water/sewer?
  6. Mortgage, liens, back taxes on either parcel?
  7. Price anchors — “Had it appraised? Gotten offers before? What number makes it worth selling?” (Developers likely already mailed them.)
  8. Close: schedule a specific callback day. No number quoted until Deal Analyzer MAO exists.

5 · Homework before the offer call

  • State property viewer (tnmap.tn.gov/assessment): look up the home parcel, then click neighboring parcels to find the vacant lot by matching owner name. Record both APNs, acreage, assessed values. (Site blocks robots — manual, ~5 min.)
  • Check if the vacant lot touches a road on its own — an interior parcel that only connects through the house parcel prices very differently and can't be split off cleanly.
  • Check greenbelt enrollment on the vacant lot (common for 30+ ac in TN). Rollback taxes (~2 yrs post-2023 law; generally buyer's responsibility since 2016, but negotiable) — price into any developer exit.
  • Assessor gaps → call the county assessor's office.
  • Pull sold comps 25–60 ac in the corridor (LandSearch, Zillow sold filter, AcreValue) → Deal Analyzer → OPEN + MAO.
  • FEMA flood + USFWS wetlands + slope on both parcels (Checklist stage B).
  • One conversation with a TN real-estate attorney / title company about assignment vs option at this deal size.
